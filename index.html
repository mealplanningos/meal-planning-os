Plan Your Week
Start here — before you think about food. A packed AM means breakfast needs to be ready to go. A busy PM means an easy dinner or planned takeout. Your schedule shapes your meals, not the other way around.
⚡ The rule: Map your week before deciding what to eat. Your schedule drives your meal choices — not the other way around. Commitments you add here will appear in your meal plan as a reminder.
Your Go-To Meals
Build your No-Decision Menu. Max 5 per meal category — that's intentional. Fewer choices, less decision fatigue. These save automatically so you only build this once.
💾 Everything saves automatically to your browser. Your recipes will be here every time you come back. Add your own or browse the Recipe Library to get started.
Recipe Library
Curated recipes from the Meal Planning OS kitchen. Browse, pick what fits your life, and add up to 5 per meal category to your Go-To Meals.
👨‍🍳
How this works: Find a recipe you like, hit "Add to My Go-Tos," and it'll appear in Step 2 with all the ingredients already loaded — ready to use in your meal plan and grocery list. You can edit any recipe after adding it.
Your Meal Plan
Click any slot to assign a meal. Your week's commitments show above each day so you can plan around them. Type any meal name, choose a category, or pick from your go-to recipes — whatever's fastest.
Grocery List
Auto-built from your meal plan. Sorted by store section. Check off as you shop. Your staples and backup items are always at the bottom.
❄️ Freezer Inventory
Your freezer is a productivity tool. Log extra portions when you batch cook. Pull frozen meals into your plan on busy nights. Oldest meals show first — that's FIFO.
Frozen Meals
FIFO Method
First In, First Out. Always eat your oldest frozen meals first. Keeps your inventory fresh and prevents waste.

The meal marked USE FIRST is oldest.

Frozen meals last 3–6 months. Always label with the date made and expiration.
